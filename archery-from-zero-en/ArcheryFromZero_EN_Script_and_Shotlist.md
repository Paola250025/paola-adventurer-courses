# ARCHERY FROM ZERO — Recording Script + B-roll Shot List
### English · Paola Adventurer · Talking-head (Loom/OBS) + b-roll · 10 lessons

**How to use this while recording:**
- Read the **SAY** lines to camera in your own rhythm — warm and personal, like you're coaching a friend.
- **ON SCREEN** = the card that matches this beat (numbers = your *Archery From Zero* cards).
- **B-ROLL** = footage over your voice — or, on Loom, hold the real gear up to the camera and show it live.
- Each lesson follows the same 5 beats: **Hook → Explain → Show → Repeat → Recap + checkpoint + next.**
- **Series anchor line — say it in every recap:** *"It doesn't have to be perfect. It has to happen."*
- Safety note: film a correct version and, only when safe, an exaggerated "wrong" version to compare — never film unsafe handling as entertainment.

## LESSON 2 — Safety First (~3–3:30 min)

**[Card 2 · Safety notice]** Show the Safety card while you say the hook.
**[Card 5 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 2: Safety First*

- **SAY (hook):** "Safety comes before aim, before speed, before hitting the center. These rules aren't here to scare you — they're here so you can enjoy this with total peace of mind."
- **SAY (explain):** "Only point the bow and arrow toward the target. Always know what's behind your target. And if something feels unsafe, you stop — no exceptions."
- **B-ROLL (show):** Target with a safe backstop and clear area; the shooting line. *"Look at this: the target, a safe backstop behind it, and everyone stays behind this line until it's their turn."*

**[Card 6 · The 3 rules]**
- ON SCREEN: *The three rules — point only at the target · know what's behind · feels unsafe? stop*
- **SAY (repeat):** "When you're not shooting, the bow goes down, like this. Before you shoot any arrow, check it for cracks, bends, or loose parts. To collect arrows: wait, call out that it's clear, walk — never run — and pull them out carefully from the side."
- **SAY (recap + close):** "Review these rules again if you're practicing with someone new or with kids. With this, you already have the most important part of the sport. Next video, we build your practice space. It doesn't have to be perfect — it has to happen."
- **B-ROLL:** Bow-down position; checking an arrow for damage; safe retrieval sequence.

## LESSON 3 — Build Your Safe Practice Space (~2:30–3 min)

**[Card 7 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 3: Your Practice Space*

- **SAY (hook):** "The target matters, but what's behind the target matters even more. I'll show you how to build a safe practice space in minutes — you don't need a professional range, just a simple space that's well thought out."
- **SAY (explain):** "Start close: ten to fifteen yards is plenty for a beginner. Create a clear shooting line, and check the area every single time you practice, not just the first time."
- **B-ROLL (show):** Measuring distance; marking the shooting line. *"Here's how you measure: ten to fifteen yards with steps or tape. And here's how you mark your line — cones, rope, chalk, whatever you have."*

**[Card 8 · Set it up safe]**
- ON SCREEN: *Start close 10–15 yd · clear shooting line · safe backstop · scan every time*
- **SAY (repeat):** "Check that your target is straight and stable, not tipping. Before you shoot, scan: left, right, behind the target, and behind you. No people, pets, streets, or houses anywhere behind the target."
- **SAY (recap + close):** "Set this space up once and take a photo, so you can check it quickly next time. Next video, the full 8-step shot cycle. It doesn't have to be perfect — it has to happen."
- **B-ROLL:** Stable target; scanning surroundings; clear background.

## LESSON 4 — The 8-Step Shot Cycle (~3–3:30 min)

**[Card 9 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 4: The 8-Step Cycle*

- **SAY (hook):** "Every shot follows the same simple rhythm. Learn this order and you've already solved eighty percent of the process. Come back to this one anytime you forget the steps."
- **SAY (explain):** "Stance, grip, nock, draw, anchor, aim, release, follow through. That's the full cycle. Don't rush it — smooth and repeatable is the goal, not speed."
- **B-ROLL (show):** One full shot, slow, full body, then from the side. *"Watch this full shot slowly, so you see how each step connects to the next."*

**[Card 10 · The 8 steps]**

- **B-ROLL:** Version pausing at each step; then 3 arrows at normal speed; arrow grouping.

## LESSON 5 — Stance & Grip (~3 min)

**[Card 11 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 5: Stance & Grip*

- **SAY (hook):** "Your stance is your foundation. If you feel wobbly or inconsistent, it almost always starts here — with how you stand. And stop fighting the bow: a relaxed grip changes everything."
- **SAY (explain):** "Stand sideways to the target, feet about shoulder-width, balanced and relaxed. In your bow hand: don't squeeze it like a hammer — let the grip rest naturally."
- **B-ROLL (show):** Front and side stance; hand on the grip. *"Look at this stance: sideways, feet aligned, balanced. And this hand: relaxed, not squeezing."*

**[Card 12 · Build your base]**
- ON SCREEN: *Sideways · shoulder-width · relaxed grip · same shot every time*
- **SAY (repeat):** "Here's the most common stance mistake — feet too close or the body twisting. And the fix: stable and relaxed. Same with the grip: a tight fist is the mistake, a relaxed hand is the fix. A relaxed hand lets the bow move the same way every time."
- **SAY (recap + close):** "Next time, before you draw, check just two things: your sideways stance and your relaxed hand. Next video, we nock, draw, and find your anchor. It doesn't have to be perfect — it has to happen."
- **B-ROLL:** Wrong vs. correct comparison for stance and grip.

## LESSON 6 — Nock, Draw & Anchor (~3:30 min)

**[Card 13 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 6: Nock, Draw & Anchor*

- **SAY (hook):** "This is the step that makes every shot feel the same instead of different every time. If your arrows land all over the target, the problem is almost always right here — at your anchor point."
- **SAY (explain):** "Nocking means placing the arrow on the string — only once you're at the line, ready to shoot. Draw smoothly, not quickly, keeping the bow pointed at the target. Your anchor is the exact spot your hand returns to, every time."
- **B-ROLL (show):** Arrow on the rest, nock clicking on; smooth side-view draw.

**[Card 14 · Same way every time]**
- ON SCREEN: *Nock at the line · draw smooth & level · anchor same spot · that's what makes arrows group*
- **SAY (repeat):** "Here's the mistake: drawing too high or pointing upward. The fix: a level draw, straight at the target. Now watch my hand land on the exact same anchor point near my face, again and again. That repeated spot is what makes your shots group."
- **SAY (recap + close):** "Pick your anchor — the corner of your mouth or your jaw — and use it exactly the same every shot. Next video, aiming and releasing, and it's simpler than you think. It doesn't have to be perfect — it has to happen."
- **B-ROLL:** High vs. level draw comparison; anchor repeated 3 times.

## LESSON 7 — Aim & Release (~3 min)

**[Card 15 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 7: Aim & Release*

- **SAY (hook):** "Aiming matters, but repeatable form matters first. And your follow-through tells you whether you stayed with the shot, or abandoned it halfway."
- **SAY (explain):** "Look at the target, stay steady, and repeat the same process every time. Don't chase perfection on your first practice. When you release: relax your fingers, let the string go, and hold your position one extra second after the arrow's gone."
- **B-ROLL (show):** Over-the-shoulder view of the target; arrow landing.

**[Card 16 · Let the shot happen]**
- ON SCREEN: *Look at target · relax fingers · hold follow-through · chase a group*
- **SAY (repeat):** "Watch this in slow motion: the fingers relax, they don't pull. And the comparison — yanking your hand away or dropping the bow is the mistake. Holding still, with a smooth, quiet follow-through, is the fix."
- **SAY (recap + close):** "Hold onto one thing: after you release, don't move for one extra second. Next video, we put it all together in your first real practice. It doesn't have to be perfect — it has to happen."
- **B-ROLL:** Slow-motion release; wrong vs. correct comparison.

## LESSON 8 — Your First Practice: 3 Arrows at a Time (~3–3:30 min)

**[Card 17 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 8: Your First Practice*

- **SAY (hook):** "This is your first real practice, guided start to finish, with a simple routine that feels doable. Short, focused practice beats long practice with bad habits."
- **SAY (explain):** "Shoot three arrows at a time, then retrieve them safely. Pick one focus per round — for example, your stance or your anchor — not everything at once."
- **B-ROLL (show):** Setting 3 arrows at the line; shooting the group slowly.

**[Card 18 · Your routine]**
- ON SCREEN: *Shoot 3, retrieve safely · one focus per round · jot a note · 15–20 min*
- **SAY (repeat):** "Retrieve using the same safety rules you know. And here's something small that makes a real difference: jot a quick note after each round — what felt good, what to adjust. Then reset for another round."
- **SAY (recap + close):** "Your first practice doesn't need to be long — fifteen to twenty minutes with this three-arrow rhythm is plenty. Next video, the most common beginner mistakes. It doesn't have to be perfect — it has to happen."
- **B-ROLL:** Safe retrieval; notebook note; resetting.

## LESSON 9 — Common Beginner Mistakes (~3 min)

**[Card 19 · Title]** ON SCREEN: *Lesson 9: Common Mistakes*

- **SAY (hook):** "If your arrows aren't going where you want, it doesn't mean you're bad at this. It's almost always one small thing."
- **SAY (explain):** "Most beginner problems come from just one part of the process, not everything at once. Fix one thing, practice, then move to the next."
- **B-ROLL (show):** Side-by-side comparisons: grip, anchor, feet, speed.

**[Card 20 · Fix the small things]**
- ON SCREEN: *Tight grip → relax · moving after release → hold · inconsistent anchor → same spot · rushing → slow the cycle*
- **SAY (repeat):** "A tight grip versus a relaxed one. Moving after release versus holding your follow-through. An inconsistent anchor versus the same spot every time. And rushing the whole cycle versus doing it slowly, step by step, like in Lesson 4."
- **SAY (recap + close):** "Next time something's off, check this list and fix just one thing at a time. Next video, if you practice with kids, there's a lesson made just for that. It doesn't have to be perfect — it has to happen."
- **B-ROLL:** Unsafe high draw vs. level draw; rushed vs. slow cycle.
